RUNNING TIPS
WiRun

Prepare for a Marathon:

- Run 3-4 times per week.
- Gradually increase your distance.
- Beginners should aim to comfortably run 3-5 miles.
- Mix short and long runs to build endurance, speed, and strength.
- Include rest days to recover.
- Create a training plan, typically lasting 16-20 weeks.
- Focus on core, legs, and glutes to improve running performance.
- Eat a balanced and nutritious diet.
- Drink enough water to stay hydrated.
- Be mentally prepared. Set a mindset that will help you reach the finish line. If it works for you, maintain a positive outlook and enjoy the experience of running.
- Practice your race day routine, such as waking up early as if it’s the actual marathon day.
- Two weeks before the marathon, taper by reducing your mileage to let your body recover.
- On marathon day, start at a comfortable pace. Don’t run too fast early on.
- Have a fuel and hydration plan.

Running Form:

- Keep your head up, looking forward, with your chin parallel to the ground. Avoid looking at your feet.
- Relax your shoulders, keeping them low and loose. Avoid hunching and tensing.
- Keep your torso upright, leaning forward from your ankles, not your waist. This helps maintain momentum and balance.
- Bend your elbows to a 90-degree angle, swinging from your shoulders, not elbows. Move your arms forward and backward, not across your body.
- Relax your hands as if holding a chip without crushing it.
- Avoid overstriding, where your foot lands too far in front of your body. Make short, quicker strides that allow your foot to land close to your center of gravity.
- Aim for midfoot or forefoot strikes instead of heel strikes to reduce impact forces.
- Aim for a cadence (steps per minute) of 170-180.
- Keep your hips stable, avoiding side-to-side movement.
- Drive your knees forward with each step, staying in line with your feet. Avoid excessive knee lifts.
- Find a comfortable breathing rhythm, typically inhaling for two steps and exhaling for two. Breathe from your diaphragm (belly breathing), not your chest.
- Keep your whole body relaxed, including your face, and keep your mouth slightly open.
- Wear appropriate running shoes for your runs.
